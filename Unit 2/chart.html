High disparities amongst Staten Island distracted driving accidents

By Christine DeRosa

Since the beginning of 2019, more than 3,000 vehicular accidents have occurred on Staten Island, according to data from the NYPD.

The dataset was created from accidents reported to the NYPD via police reports, which are required to be filled out for collisions where someone is injured or killed, or where there is at least $1000 worth of damage.

Over 800 accidents in the borough were caused by distracted driving. Hand-held cell phone use was not the main cause of these accidents.

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration spokesperson Jose Ucles said that eyes should be on the road and hands on the wheel all the time. No one call or text is worth a life, injuring someone or oneself or a crash.
"To prevent tragedies due to distracted driving, motorists are urged to turn off electronic devices and put them out of reach before starting to drive, be good role models for young drivers and set a good example and talk with your teens about responsible driving," Ucles said.
Ucles also urged passengers to speak up when your driver uses an electronic device and offer to make the call for the driver so his attention stays on the road. Also, you should always wear your seat belt, which is the best defense against unsafe drivers.
Additionally, all pedestrians and bicyclists should focus on their surroundings and not on their electronic devices.
